Bio #1

Ruby is one of the designers developing the visual material for Loose Leaf. Focusing on the publication of the exhibition, Ruby’s intent was to convey the core ideals of the group exhibition within a striking piece of visual communication design. The publication is careful and considered interplay between the accurate portrayal of the exhibiting artists and crafting of a cohesive design output. The loose leaves of paper, allowed to be rearranged, hand crafted appeal and paired back visual style are are a few of the considered elements in the design output.

Ruby has a keen interest in editorial design, thus the Loose Leaf exhibition is an ideal platform for her to play out her skills. Aside from this she also practices within illustration and interactive design fields, with the aim of creating powerful visual experiences.

Bio #2

Alfred is a graphic designer who’s main role was in the design of the website for Loose Leaf. His intention is to increase the longevity of the exhibtion while seeing how the themes and aesthetics of the exhibition could translate into the digital space. Alongside this, he supported other aspects of the exhibtion design, including the photography, printing and poster development.

As a designer, Alfred has mainly been interested in code and the digital world, however, he has still gone into print every so often. At his core, Alfred’s ethos is to be experimental and to design for memorable experiences, regardless of the medium.
