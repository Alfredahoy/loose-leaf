Bio #1

Ruby is a graphic design who developed the print material for Loose Leaf. Focusing on the publication design of the exhibition, Ruby’s intent was to convey the group's core ideals into a visually engaging design output. The loose leaves of paper, hand crafted appeal and pared back visual style are a few of the considered elements in the design output. The publication is a careful interplay between the accurate portrayal of the exhibiting artists and the formation of the exhibition’s identity.

Ruby has a keen interest in editorial design, thus the Loose Leaf exhibition catalogue is an ideal platform for her to play out her skills. Aside from this she also practices within the fields of illustration and interactive design, with the aim of creating powerful visual experiences.

Bio #2

Alfred is a graphic designer who’s main role was in the design of the website for Loose Leaf. His intention was to increase the longevity of the exhibtion while seeing how the themes and aesthetics of the exhibition could translate into the digital space. Alongside this, he supported other aspects of the exhibition design, including the photography, printing and poster development.

As a designer, Alfred has mainly been interested in code and the digital world, however, he has still gone into print every so often. At his core, Alfred’s ethos is to be experimental and to design for memorable experiences, regardless of the medium.
