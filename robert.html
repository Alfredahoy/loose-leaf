Bio #1

Although his wider interests are expansive and expanding, Robert’s practice is primarily text-based. Because the communication of ideas through the use of words is already a ubiquitous pursuit, he is interested in finding novel adaptations of various strategies which exist. This provides opportunities to explore the conventions of conventional media. Robert makes playful use of these, ranging from the grammatical, lexical, and conceptual, to the physical.

This results in strings of words not too dissimilar from poetry. Despite this, he is wary of referring to himself as a poet. In a general sense, his conceptual works relate more to linguistics and sculpture, than they do to writing.

Bio #2

One of his default processes has been to subject simple ideas to a process of over-complication, whereupon he sets himself the difficult task of making the concepts accessible for a wider audience. In recent times, he has been formulating titles before bringing them into existence.

Robert’s practice is heavily research based, as he wishes to highlight nuanced relationships between disparate contexts. This further influences his interest in words and aesthetic ideation.
