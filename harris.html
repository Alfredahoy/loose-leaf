Bio #1

Harris uses the intrinsic, behavioral qualities of paint to develop an abstract painting methodology that seeks to challenge perceptions of space around and within the canvas. Treating anamorphosis as a tool, he manipulates and reproduces simple geometric shapes through a combination of the projection of light and the distortion of form.

Drawing upon colour theory, he plans the composition of shape and colour to emphasize experiential, embodied aspects of perception, and to explore the illusion of depth within a two-dimensional surface. The application of paint and arrangement of elements concentrates upon locations where edges meet to generate tension, energy, and dialogue.

Bio #2

Conceptually speaking, his interests are grounded in the undertaken research of the fourth dimension and its mysteries surrounding the notion of time in relation to space. There is, however, no singular definition of the fourth dimension. This has provided opportunity for him to form his own process and method of utilizing anamorphosis and colour theory as his tools to understanding it.

It is by no means, however, that he ever intends on aspiring to visually present the fourth dimension due to its logical limitations. Instead, he merely intends on producing paintings that open a discussion around the concepts that it builds on.
