Bio #1

Polly’s art practice has evolved through a rigorous process based exploration; drawing from a multiplicity of artistic traditions from painting, sculpture, installation and sewing. Her conceptual ideas are distinguished by a colour scheme made up of rarefied light and atmospheres.

Referring to herself as a painter with no hesitation, her previous works over the last 2 years rarely if ever, had taken the shape of a traditional canvas on stretcher format. From this, Polly soon began reinterpreting undertones of abstract expressionism, and translating it into a contemporary manner, essentially seeking to present painting as an object rather than a 2-dimensional surface.

Bio #2

Fundamentally Polly’s concepts rely on stripping back materials and objects to highlight their aesthetic potential. Works that she intends to stand out for their cold environment like colors. They are cohesive with the cold space they inhabit, accompanying the viewer towards an evocative atmosphere and seemingly aseptic, emotionless light.

Framing is a traditional element that Polly employs in her works. Polly tends to explore the importance of the ‘frame’, creating compositions which cross it and continue inside and outside it, resulting in the breaking of paintings ‘limitations’. Primarily questioning pre-conceptual thoughts of modern abstraction, allowing viewers to delve deep into the roots of art and question not only how art is constructed, but why artists create art that portrays the beginnings of creation.
